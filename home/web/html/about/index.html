Overview

The world is moving towards becoming entirely driven by technology. Every business must now embrace the idea of becoming a technology company. Making this change is hard. Maintaining a competitive edge in this day and age is becoming increasingly difficult.

Our ability to deliver a service can grind to a halt as we try scale with existing inefficient platforms, processes and structure. Decade old technology companies have undergone these scaling pains and most have used the same methods to overcome those challenges.

With the emergence of the Cloud we've accelerated software delivery and unlocked elasticity in infrastructure management through APIs but software development tools have lagged behind.

At Micro we think we've found the model for effectively leveraging the Cloud and now want to share that with the world.


Design

Microservices is a software design pattern which accelerates development by breaking down services into smaller units of composition which can be worked on and delivered independently.

The concept of microservices is not new, it's the reimagining of service based development as an approach more holistically aligned with the unix philosophy of creating small processes that do one thing well, which can then be combined via their inputs and outputs.


The philosophy of microservices:

  • Services are small - fine-grained as a singular business purpose similar to unix philosophy of "Do one thing and do it well"

  • The organization culture should embrace automation of deployment and testing. This eases the burden on management and operations

  • The culture and design principles should embrace failure and faults, similar to anti-fragile systems.

We believe microservices development is the key driving force of innovation in companies like Amazon, Google and Facebook today. And now we want to share that with you.


Architecture

As companies scale growth in usage and adoption, technology and people within the company become a blocker of innovation and progress. One example was the explosive growth of Twitter which led to a number of issues in 2009 and the fail whale we'd all become so accustomed to.

Microservices enabled Twitter to breakout the product into smaller services which teams could work on and scale independently of each other. Each team became responsible for the delivery of their particular service which others may come to use through common APIs.


We've seen through first hand experience that microservice systems enable faster development cycles, improved productivity and superior scalable systems.

Let's talk about some of the benefits:

  • 1. Easier to scale development - teams organise around different business requirements and manage their own services.
  • 2. Easier to understand - microservices are much smaller, usually 1000 LOC or less.
  • 3. Easier to deploy new versions of services frequently - services can be deployed, scaled and managed independently.
  • 4. Improved fault tolerance and isolation - separation of concerns minimises the impact of issues in one service from another.
  • 5. Improved speed of execution - teams deliver on business requirements faster by developing, deploying and managing microservices independently.
  • 6. Reusable services and rapid prototyping - the unix philosophy ingrained in microservices allow you to reuse existing services and build entirely new functionality on top much quicker.
